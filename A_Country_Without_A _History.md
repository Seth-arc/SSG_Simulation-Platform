# A Country with No History — Facilitator Pack (Ready-to-Print)

Below is a **ready-to-print facilitator pack** for **A Country with No History** (in-person simulation). It’s built to directly embody your image: **DATA** at the base → **interconnected systems**, **multiple stakeholders**, **non-linear relationships**, **uncertainty/ambiguity**, **different interests**, and **feedback loops**.

---

## 0) At-a-glance

**Runtime:** 90 minutes (works at 60/120)  
**Players:** 8–24 (best: 12–18)  
**Group structure:** 1 National Council + 3 Pods (Writing/Narrative, Visual/Design, Media)  
**Core mechanic:** 10 Policy Tokens per round + contested data packets + non-linear system response

---

## 1) Facilitator setup

### Materials (print)
- Role Cards (one per participant)
- **Dashboard Poster** (6 indicators with 0–10 track)
- Policy Tokens (paper squares or poker chips) — **10 per round**
- Round Packets (Round 1, Round 2, Round 3)
- Stakeholder Pressure Chips (optional) — 2 per role
- “Founding Charter” sheet (1 per group)
- “Policy Revision Memo” sheet (1 per group)
- “Resilience Plan + Measurement Plan” sheet (1 per group)

### Room layout
- Big wall/whiteboard = **National Dashboard**
- Central table = **National Council** (decision + tokens)
- 3 side tables = Pods: **Writing/Narrative**, **Visual/Design**, **Media**
- A “Gallery Wall” where pods post outputs each round

### Rules you say out loud (script)
1) “In Nohistoria, **there is no precedent**. You cannot argue ‘we’ve always done it this way.’”
2) “Your job is to **govern with contested data** and conflicting incentives.”
3) “Every decision **creates feedback**. Outcomes will not be linear.”
4) “You must publish something each round: **a narrative, a plan, and a data choice**.”

## 2) The Dashboard

Print this as a big poster and update with marker each round.

### National Dashboard (0–10)
1) **Trust / Legitimacy**  
2) **Economic Activity**  
3) **Public Health**  
4) **Food & Water Security**  
5) **Environmental Stability**

Write as **ranges** (uncertainty). You can reveal exact values later.

- Trust: **4–6**
- Economy: **3–6**
- Health: **4–7**
- Food/Water: **5–7**
- Environment: **4–6**
- Capacity: **2–5**

## 3) Core mechanics

### Policy Tokens
Each round the Council receives **10 tokens** to allocate across 5 baskets:

1) **Basic Services** (health, water, safety nets)  
2) **Jobs & Markets** (trade, industry, investment)  
3) **Data & Institutions** (statistics office, civil service, procurement, audits)  
4) **Social Cohesion** (community programs, rights, inclusion, civic comms)  
5) **Resilience & Environment** (drought prep, land, infrastructure resilience)

**Hard constraint:** Must spend all 10 tokens.

### Output per round
- **Round 1:** Founding Charter + token allocation + “Truth Standard” choice
- **Round 2:** Revision Memo + token reallocation + “What data do we trust now?”
- **Round 3:** Resilience Plan + measurement plan + final dashboard story

### Pod outputs (fast, tangible)
- **Writing/Narrative:** 120–180 word “National Update” each round
- **Visual/Design:** 1-page dashboard sketch / infographic / flow map
- **Media:** 30-second script + headline + 3 bullet “what the public hears”

---

## 4) Hidden system response sheet (non-linear rules)

Keep this behind your clipboard. You update the dashboard after each round.

### A) Token → effect (base effects)
For each basket, apply:

- **Basic Services:** +Health, +Food/Water (small), +Trust (if Health improves)
- **Jobs & Markets:** +Economy, +Trust (if economy improves), but can harm Environment if neglected
- **Data & Institutions:** +Capacity, reduces uncertainty next round
- **Social Cohesion:** +Trust, stabilizes stakeholder behavior
- **Resilience & Environment:** +Environment, +Food/Water (if drought risk), prevents shock amplification

#### Simple mapping (per token)
Use these deltas as a baseline:
- Basic Services: +0.5 Health, +0.3 Food/Water
- Jobs & Markets: +0.6 Economy
- Data & Institutions: +0.7 Capacity
- Social Cohesion: +0.7 Trust
- Resilience & Environment: +0.6 Environment, +0.2 Food/Water

(You’ll round at the end.)

### B) Thresholds (non-linear)
1) **Trust < 3:** Next round you only get **9 tokens** (implementation friction).  
2) **Capacity < 3:** Next round data gets worse (more contradictions, wider ranges).  
3) **Environment < 3:** Food/Water drops **-1** next round automatically.  
4) **Economy > 7 while Environment < 5:** Environment drops **-1** (growth externality).  
5) **Food/Water < 4:** Trust drops **-1** (people panic, rumors grow).

### C) Feedback loops (policy → behavior → system)
- If **Data & Institutions ≥ 3 tokens** this round: next round ranges narrow (uncertainty reduces).
- If **Social Cohesion = 0 tokens**: trigger 1 stakeholder action card next round automatically.
- If **Basic Services = 0 tokens**: Health drops **-1** next round (even if other areas improve).

### D) Stakeholder escalation (ignored twice)
If a role’s “Must Fund” priority is ignored in **two rounds**, they trigger an **Action Card** immediately.

## 5) Role Cards (print and cut)

Give one to each participant. If fewer people, combine roles.

### 1) Prime Coordinator (Chair)
**You want:** A plan that passes; legitimacy; speed  
**You fear:** paralysis + reputational collapse  
**Must fund:** At least 1 token in **Data & Institutions** OR **Social Cohesion** each round  
**Leverage move:** Call for vote; propose compromise package

### 2) Finance Ministry
**You want:** budget discipline, investor confidence, clean procurement  
**Must fund:** 2 tokens in **Jobs & Markets** OR 2 in **Data & Institutions**  
**Red line:** “Unfunded promises” and “opaque spending”  
**If ignored:** “Freeze releases” (reduce next round tokens by 1)

### 3) Health Ministry
**You want:** clinics, outbreak readiness, workforce  
**Must fund:** 2 tokens **Basic Services**  
**Red line:** Cutting services while publishing optimistic narratives  
**If ignored:** “Emergency declaration” (Trust -1 if not addressed next round)

### 4) Education & Culture
**You want:** shared identity, literacy, national story people believe  
**Must fund:** 1 token **Social Cohesion**  
**Red line:** fractured narrative that fuels division  
**If ignored:** “Competing histories emerge” (Trust -1, Media chaos)

### 5) Trade & Industry
**You want:** jobs now, simplified regulations, trade access  
**Must fund:** 2 tokens **Jobs & Markets**  
**Red line:** bureaucracy strangling growth  
**If ignored:** “Capital flight rumor” (Economy -1 unless cohesion + capacity are high)

### 6) Environment & Land
**You want:** water security, land stability, hazard prevention  
**Must fund:** 2 tokens **Resilience & Environment**  
**Red line:** growth that wrecks long-term survivability  
**If ignored:** “Drought warnings” (Food/Water -1 next round)

### 7) Local Regions Council
**You want:** equitable distribution, regional voice, infrastructure  
**Must fund:** 1 token **Basic Services** AND 1 token **Social Cohesion** over 2 rounds  
**Red line:** capital-city bias  
**If ignored:** “Regional non-cooperation” (Capacity -1 next round)

### 8) Civil Society Coalition
**You want:** rights, transparency, accountability, trust  
**Must fund:** 1 token **Social Cohesion** OR 2 tokens **Data & Institutions**  
**Red line:** secrecy or coercive messaging  
**If ignored:** “Public trust campaign” (Trust fluctuates; add contradiction to data packet)

### 9) Private Sector Chamber
**You want:** speed, clarity, stable rules  
**Must fund:** 1 token **Jobs & Markets**  
**Red line:** uncertain rules + unpredictable enforcement  
**If ignored:** “Investor delay” (Economy +0 this round regardless of tokens in Jobs)

### 10) International Partners
**You want:** measurable outcomes, safeguards, optics  
**Must fund:** 2 tokens **Data & Institutions** OR 2 tokens **Basic Services**  
**Red line:** money without measurement  
**If ignored:** “Disbursement pause” (remove 2 tokens next round unless fixed)

#### Optional roles (if 11–12+)
**11) Media / Narratives Desk**  
Can publish a headline each round that shifts Trust by ±1 depending on alignment with reality.  
**12) Data Authority (Stats Office)**  
Chooses what counts as “official.” Can narrow ranges if funded; can also expose “revisions.”

## 6) Round Packets (print as envelopes)

Each packet includes: **Data Cards + Rumor Cards + Event Prompt + Tasks**.

### Round 1 Packet: “Founding”
#### Event prompt (read aloud)
“Nohistoria is recognized. No shared history. No trusted numbers. Your first act is to define what is true and what you will measure.”

#### Data Cards (hand to Council)
1) **Official Estimate:** Trust 6, Economy 5, Capacity 4 (confidence low)  
2) **Field Reports:** Regions say trust is “thin”; queues at clinics growing  
3) **Proxy Signal:** Night lights suggest economy is closer to 3 than 5  
4) **Satellite/Climate:** Rainfall normal, but soil moisture uneven

#### Rumor Cards (give to Media pod + Civil Society)
- “The capital is hiding the real numbers.”
- “International partners will only fund what can be measured.”

#### Round 1 Task Sheet
1) Choose **3 National Values** (words only).  
2) Choose **Truth Standard** (pick ONE):
   - A) “Official Statistics first”
   - B) “Field reality first”
   - C) “Proxies first”
   - D) “Triangulation” (slower, more credible)
3) Allocate **10 tokens** across 5 baskets.  
4) Produce outputs:
   - Writing: National Update (120–180 words)
   - Visual: Dashboard sketch showing your priorities
   - Media: Headline + 30-sec script

#### Facilitator resolution (after they decide)
Apply token effects + thresholds. Then set dashboard to **rounded** values but keep uncertainty visible (still ranges next round unless Data & Institutions ≥ 3).

---

### Round 2 Packet: “Consequences + Conflicting Realities”
#### Event prompt
“Week 6. Your first policies landed. Now your numbers get revised. Stakeholders disagree on what the country even is.”

#### Data Cards
1) **Revision Notice:** previous Trust was overstated by 1–2 points.  
2) **Health Signal:** clinic attendance up; one region reports a respiratory spike (uncertain).  
3) **Economy Signal:** small business activity up, but imports cost more (currency pressure).  
4) **Governance Signal:** procurement delays reported; people blame “new rules.”

#### Stakeholder Trigger Cards (use if someone ignored twice)
Draw or assign based on tension:
- Finance: “Audit demand” (must add 2 tokens to Data & Institutions next round or Trust -1)
- Regions: “Implementation slowdown” (Capacity -1 next round)
- Civil Society: “Transparency campaign” (add contradiction card next round)
- Private sector: “Investor pause” (Economy -1 unless Jobs & Markets ≥ 3)

#### Round 2 Mini-activity: TRUTH COURT (5–7 min)
Two roles present opposite interpretations (Official vs Field vs Proxy).
Council votes:
- accept one
- or invest in measurement (Data & Institutions ≥ 3 next round gives “cleaner” packet)

#### Round 2 Task Sheet
1) Publish a **Policy Revision Memo** (1 page).  
2) Re-allocate **10 tokens**.  
3) Decide: “What data is authoritative this round?” (one sentence)  
4) Outputs:
   - Writing: Revised National Update
   - Visual: “Cause → Effect map” (show one feedback loop)
   - Media: headline + 30-sec script

#### Facilitator resolution
Apply token effects. If Trust dropped below 3, next round starts with 9 tokens. If Capacity < 3, widen uncertainty next round.

---

### Round 3 Packet: “Shock + Adaptation”
Choose ONE shock (pick the one that best fits your cohort).

#### Shock A: Drought Pulse
Rain fails in 2 regions. Food/water risk rises fast.

#### Shock B: Disease Uptick
Not confirmed outbreak—but rumors spread and behavior changes.

#### Shock C: Commodity Whiplash
Export prices swing; economy unstable; budget stress.

#### Shock D: Misinformation Wave
Fake “leaked stats” circulate; trust collapses unless addressed.

#### Data Cards (depending on shock)
Give 2 of these plus 1 contradiction:
- “Satellite shows drought in north”
- “Hospital reports are inconsistent”
- “Market prices spiking”
- “Social media panic index high”
- Contradiction: “Official statement denies severity”

#### Round 3 Task Sheet
1) Create a **Resilience Plan**:
   - 3 immediate actions
   - 2 short-term reforms
   - 1 measurement commitment (what you’ll track weekly)
2) Allocate tokens (you may only have 9 if Trust < 3).  
3) Outputs:
   - Writing: “Address to the Nation”
   - Visual: “Resilience dashboard” (what you’ll measure)
   - Media: headline + 30-sec script + “what we won’t claim”

#### Facilitator resolution
Apply thresholds and finalize dashboard. Then transition to debrief.

## 8) Debrief guide (maps to your image)

Put your image up and run these questions:

1) **Interconnected Systems:** “Where did a ‘good’ decision create a problem elsewhere?”  
2) **Multiple Stakeholders:** “Who shaped outcomes the most—policy or stakeholder behavior?”  
3) **Non-Linear Relationships:** “Where did you see thresholds or sudden swings?”  
4) **Uncertainty & Ambiguity:** “How did you decide what was real?”  
5) **Different interests & focus:** “What tradeoff was hardest to accept?”  
6) **Feedback loops:** “What did you change after new evidence arrived?”  
7) **DATA as foundation:** “What would you institutionalize so Round 4 is easier?”

Finish with:
- “Write one sentence: *In complex systems, data is not truth; it is…*”

## 9) Difficulty settings (choose one)

### Intro (most groups)
- Use baseline token effects only + 1 threshold (Trust < 3 → 9 tokens)

### Intermediate
- Use all thresholds + stakeholder escalation after 2 ignores

### Brutal (use for advanced cohorts)
- Start Capacity at **2**
- Every round includes 1 contradiction
- If Data & Institutions < 2 for two rounds, impose “statistical blackout” (no numbers, only narratives)
